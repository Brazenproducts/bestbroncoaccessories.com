Best Bronco Roof Accessories of 2026

Roof racks, cargo baskets, and roof-top tents — maximize your Bronco's rooftop real estate.

The Bronco's high ground clearance and wide roof make it an excellent candidate for roof-mounted gear. Whether you need extra cargo capacity for camping gear, a platform for recovery boards, or a base for a roof-top tent, there is a strong aftermarket supporting the 2021+ Bronco with purpose-built solutions.

Front Runner, Yakima, and Rhino-Rack are the established leaders in this space. Each has a distinct philosophy — Front Runner goes for a ultra-rugged expedition aesthetic, Yakima bridges OEM and aftermarket cleanly, and Rhino-Rack offers the most versatile and accessible range. For roof-top tents, iKamper and Tepui dominate the premium market.

1. Front Runner Roof Rack Systems

Editor's Choice — Expedition Grade

Front Runner Bronco Roof Rack Kit

★★★★★ 4.9/5 (premium expedition-grade)
Premium — built for serious overlanding

Front Runner makes the most robust Bronco-specific roof rack system available. The slim profile keeps wind noise manageable while the extruded aluminum slats provide serious load capacity for extended overlanding trips. Their wolf pack and jerry can mounts integrate cleanly. If you are outfitting a Bronco for serious backcountry travel, Front Runner is the clear benchmark.

✓ Pros

  • Best-in-class load capacity and durability
  • Slim aerodynamic profile
  • Full ecosystem of expedition accessories
  • Specific Bronco fitment — not generic

✗ Cons

  • Highest price point in the category
  • Overkill for casual weekend use
  • Heavier than some alternatives

2. Yakima Roof Rack Systems

Best All-Round

Yakima Bronco-Specific Roof Rack

★★★★☆ 4.7/5
Mid to premium

Yakima bridges the gap between premium expedition gear and accessible daily-use racks better than anyone. Their Bronco-specific roof rack system uses the StreamLine platform with a clean install that does not require drilling. The LoadWarrior basket and SkyRise tent systems integrate seamlessly. For owners who want serious capability without the full expedition price tag, Yakima is the right answer.

✓ Pros

  • Clean, non-drill installation
  • Wide range of compatible accessories
  • Good balance of price, capability, and aesthetics
  • Strong brand reputation and warranty

✗ Cons

  • Load capacity below Front Runner
  • Aerodynamics not quite as refined

Best Value

Rhino-Rack Bronco Roof Rack & Cargo Basket

★★★★☆ 4.5/5
Mid-range

Rhino-Rack is the most accessible of the three major brands, with a broad product range that covers everything from basic cargo baskets to full expedition platforms. Their Pioneer platform andBatwing awning combos are particularly popular with the Bronco crowd. 4. Roof-Top Tents — iKamper & Tepui

Best Camp Upgrade

iKamper Skycamp & Tepui Bronco Roof-Top Tents

★★★★★ 4.8/5 (roof-top tent category)
Premium — significant investment

Roof-top tents are the ultimate Bronco camping upgrade. The iKamper Skycamp and Tepui Explorer series are the two most recommended brands for the Bronco specifically. Both fold out to create a comfortable sleeping platform above the vehicle, keeping you away from ground moisture, critters, and uneven terrain. The trade-off is weight, cost, and the fact that you will always know your Bronco is carrying a tent on top.

✓ Pros

  • Best sleep quality of any overland setup
  • Elevated from ground moisture and wildlife
  • Quick setup vs ground tents
  • Works on any vehicle with a roof rack

✗ Cons

  • Significant added weight and wind resistance
  • Requires sturdy roof rack (Front Runner recommended)
  • Premium price — adds $1,500–$4,000 to your build
  • Cannot be removed quickly for daily driving

The Bottom Line

For serious expedition builds: Start with a Front Runner roof rack — it is the best-in-class foundation for any serious overland Bronco. Yakima is the right choice for owners who want a premium experience at a more accessible price. Rhino-Rack delivers the best value for the budget-conscious. And if you are ready to commit to the roof-top tent lifestyle, the iKamper Skycamp or Tepui Explorer are the two to beat.
