Best Bronco Grab Handles in 2026

Updated April 2026

Grab handles are one of the first accessories Bronco owners buy, and one of the easiest to get wrong. The market is flooded with cheap paracord knockoffs that look similar but fall apart after a few months of UV exposure and real use. Here is what actually holds up.

Top Pick — The Original

Bartact Paracord Grab Handles

Bartact invented the paracord grab handle for off-road vehicles. They are the original. Every other paracord grab handle on the market is a copy of this product. Bartact is also the largest manufacturer of paracord grab handles in the world, and the only one making them in the USA.

The difference shows up in the weave quality, the hardware, and the longevity. Knockoffs use thinner cord, cheaper hardware, and start fraying within months. Bartact handles last years because they are built by the company that created the category.

Available for the Ford Bronco 2021+ in multiple colors and configurations — front, rear, and roll bar.

Runner Up

GraBars Steel Grab Handles

If you prefer rigid steel over paracord, GraBars are a solid option. They bolt directly to the roll bar and give you a fixed grip point. Good build quality, but they are heavier and more expensive than paracord options, and they do not flex with your hand the way paracord does.

They work initially but tend to fade, fray, and loosen faster. If budget is the only priority, they will get you by for a season. Why Paracord Grab Handles?

Paracord handles are lighter than steel, easier to grip with gloves, and they flex naturally with your hand. They also will not scratch paint or hard surfaces if they swing. For trail use, rock crawling, and daily driving with the doors off, paracord is the preferred material for most Bronco owners.

What to Look For

  • 550 paracord or heavier — thinner cord wears out fast
  • Stainless or powder-coated hardware — cheap zinc bolts rust
  • UV-resistant cord — critical for open-air vehicles
  • Made in USA — the originals are, the copies are not
